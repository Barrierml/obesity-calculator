Note: This calculator is designed for obesity subtyping among individuals with a BMI ≥ 30 kg/m². If your BMI is unknown, please use the BMI calculator below. The model uses multinomial ridge regression trained on UK Biobank data (N=88,877). All input data is processed locally and never uploaded.

Methodology

Calculation Steps

  1. 1.Z-score normalization: z = (x − μ) / σ using UKB population parameters
  2. 2.Linear scoring: scoreₖ = β₀ₖ + Σ βᵢₖ · zᵢ
  3. 3.Softmax: P(Cₖ) = exp(scoreₖ) / Σ exp(scoreₗ)
  4. 4.Predicted subtype: argmax P(Cₖ)

Model Information

  • —Model: Multinomial Logistic Regression (L2 penalty / Ridge)
  • —Training: 88,877 UK Biobank participants
  • —Features: 7 routine clinical variables + WHR auto-derived
  • —Validation: 5-fold CV, Macro-AUC ≈ 0.89
  • —Privacy: All computation runs locally in the browser

Obesity Subtype Definitions (亚型临床特征说明)

LRO — Low-risk obesity

Characterised by moderately elevated blood pressure and HbA1c, with relatively preserved HDL-C. This subtype carries the lowest cardiometabolic risk among the five groups despite obesity.

RDO — Renal-dominant obesity

Defined by markedly elevated systolic blood pressure and serum creatinine, indicating predominant renal and vascular involvement. WHR is moderately elevated.

HGO — Hyperglycaemic obesity

Dominated by severe hyperglycaemia (very high HbA1c) and hypertriglyceridaemia, with the highest WHR. This subtype represents the most severe metabolic derangement.

HHO — High-HDL obesity

Distinguished by exceptionally high HDL cholesterol with relatively low triglycerides. Often associated with lower overall metabolic risk despite obesity.

DHO — Dyslipidaemic-hepatic obesity

Characterised by elevated triglycerides and ALT, suggesting hepatic involvement alongside dyslipidaemia. HbA1c and blood pressure tend to be moderate.
